Protein engineers routinely isolate a handful of functional binders---via phage display, yeast
surface display, or functional selections---but lack a systematic way to expand these small sets
into diverse candidate libraries without retraining a generative model. We show that a single
scalar parameter added to the softmax logits of a Hopfield-energy sampler continuously conditions
generation from ``any family member'' to ``only binders,'' with no training, no GPU, and no
architectural changes. The effectiveness of this conditioning is predicted \emph{a priori} by a
cheap geometric property of the alignment, giving practitioners a decision rule before any
sequences are generated.
