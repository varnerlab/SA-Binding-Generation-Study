\noindent\fbox{\parbox{\textwidth}{\small\textbf{Significance Statement.}
Protein engineers routinely isolate a handful of functional binders---via phage display, yeast
surface display, or functional selections---but lack a systematic way to expand these small sets
into diverse candidate libraries without retraining a generative model. We show that a single
scalar parameter added to the softmax logits of a Hopfield-energy sampler continuously conditions
generation from ``any family member'' to ``only binders,'' with no training, no GPU, and no
architectural changes. The effectiveness of this conditioning is predicted \emph{a priori} by a
cheap geometric property of the alignment, giving practitioners a decision rule before any
sequences are generated.}}

  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=0.82\textwidth]{sections/figs/fig5_entropy_curves.pdf}
  \caption{\textbf{Phase transition shifts rightward with increasing multiplicity
  ratio $\rho$.}
  Attention entropy $H(\beta)$ as a function of inverse temperature $\beta$
  (log scale) for $\rho \in \{1, 5, 20, 100, 1000\}$ on the Kunitz family.
  Each curve shows a sigmoidal drop from high entropy (exploration phase) to
  near-zero entropy (retrieval phase); dashed vertical lines mark the inflection
  point $\beta^{*}(\rho)$ for each curve.
  As $\rho$ increases, $K_{\mathrm{eff}}$ decreases and $\beta^{*}$ shifts
  rightward (from 4.3 at $\rho=1$ to 10.1 at $\rho=1000$), consistent with
  fewer effective competing attractors requiring higher inverse temperature to
  produce an ordered phase.}
  \label{fig:phase-transition}
\end{figure}
